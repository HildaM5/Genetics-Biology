Cloning

Cloning is the creation of a cell, cell product, or organism that is genetically identical to the unit or individual from which it was derived. The first mammal to be cloned from an adult cell was Dolly the sheep. It is the most famous clone in the entire world. However, cloning isn't something new because it has existed in nature since the dawn of life. A clone has the same DNA sequence as its parent so they are genetically identical. In some cultures, cloning is seemed as a sin since they believe that the only one who can give life to something should only be God. Thanks to that and some other reasons, human cloning is banned in more than 30 countries. Several European countries, including France, Germany and Switzerland, have banned the creation of cloned human embryos for reproductive or therapeutic purposes. England, Singapore, Sweden, China and Israel allow cloning for research, but prohibit it for reproduction.
